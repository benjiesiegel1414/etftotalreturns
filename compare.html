How to Compare 2 ETFs Side by Side

Comparing two ETFs is simple with this free ETF compare tool: type each ticker into the search box above (try the classic SCHD vs VYM or JEPI vs JEPQ matchups), and you'll instantly get a head-to-head view of each fund's average annual total return since inception, current yield, fund age, and grade — all on one shareable page.

Why Compare ETFs by Total Return Since Inception?

Most ETF comparison tools only show trailing 1-, 3-, or 5-year returns, which punishes newer funds and hides how a strategy has really performed over its full life. This tool annualizes each fund's total return since inception — price appreciation plus dividends — into one fair, apples-to-apples number, so you can compare a brand-new covered-call ETF against a decade-old dividend stalwart honestly.

ETF Comparison Tool — Frequently Asked Questions

How do I compare two ETFs side by side?

Type both tickers into the search box at the top of this page. The tool instantly shows a head-to-head comparison card with each ETF's average annual total return since inception, plus yield, fund age, and grade.

Can I compare more than 2 ETFs at once?

Yes — you can compare up to 8 ETFs at the same time. With 3 or more funds selected, the tool switches to a ranked leaderboard sorted by average annual return.

What is the best metric for comparing ETFs?

Average annual total return since inception is one of the fairest single numbers for comparing ETFs of different ages, because it includes both price appreciation and dividends, annualized over each fund's full lifetime.

Is this ETF comparison tool free?

Yes — 100% free, no login required. Every comparison gets its own shareable URL, and you can download the result as an image.
